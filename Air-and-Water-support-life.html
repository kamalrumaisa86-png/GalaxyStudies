📘 Science: Air and Water (Detailed Notes)

1. Composition & Properties of Air

Air is a mixture of gases (not a single substance). • Nitrogen (78%): Used by plants to make proteins. • Oxygen (21%): Necessary for breathing (respiration) and burning (combustion). • Carbon Dioxide (0.03%): Used by plants for photosynthesis. • Water Vapor & Dust: Varies by location.

Properties: 1. Occupies Space: Proven by blowing up a balloon. 2. Has Weight: A full cylinder is heavier than an empty one. 3. Exerts Pressure: Air pushes in all directions.

2. The Atmosphere & Its Layers The Atmosphere & Its Layers The atmosphere is the thick layer of air surrounding Earth. • Troposphere: Where we live and where weather happens. • Stratosphere: Contains the Ozone Layer which absorbs harmful UV rays. • Mesosphere: Protects Earth by burning up meteors. • Thermosphere: Reflects radio waves and contains the ionosphere. • Exosphere: The outermost layer merging into space.

3. Water: Why We Need It Life is impossible without water because: • It is the Universal Solvent (dissolves nutrients for our cells). • It regulates body temperature through sweating. • It is a habitat for millions of aquatic species. • Plants need it to stay upright and make food.

4. Water Purification (The Science of Clean Water) We must remove impurities to make water "potable" (safe to drink). A. Removing Insoluble Impurities (Sand/Dirt): • Sedimentation: Heavy dirt settles at the bottom of a container. • Decantation: Pouring out the clear water without disturbing the sediment. • Filtration: Passing water through a filter (sand, charcoal, or paper). B. Removing Soluble Impurities (Salts/Germs): • Evaporation: Boiling water until it turns to steam, leaving solids behind. • Distillation: Evaporating water and then condensing the steam back into a liquid. This is the purest form of water. • Boiling: Kills germs/bacteria (Must boil for at least 10 minutes). • Chlorination: Adding chlorine to kill harmful microbes in large amounts of water.

5. Points to Remember • Atmospheric Pressure helps us use a drinking straw or a syringe. • Distilled water is used in science labs and car batteries because it has no impurities. • Oxygen is the only gas in the air that supports burning. • Humidity is the amount of water vapor present in the air.
